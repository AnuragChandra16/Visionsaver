Types of Pathologies

Myopia (Nearsightedness) Myopia is a refractive error where distant objects appear blurry, while close objects can be seen clearly

Cataract Cataracts are cloudy areas in the eye's lens, leading to blurred or dim vision.

Normal Vision Normal vision refers to having clear vision at various distances without the need for corrective lenses or surgery.

Glaucoma Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions characterized by damage to the optic nerve, often due to increased pressure within the eye.

Hypertensive Retinopathy Hypertensive retinopathy is a complication of high blood pressure that affects the blood vessels in the retina.

Age-related Macular Degeneration (AMD) AMD is a progressive condition that affects the macula, the central part of the retina responsible for sharp, central vision.

Others Retinal detachment involves the retina pulling away from its normal position, causing sudden vision changes.

Healthy Tips for Good Vision

Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables to provide essential nutrients for eye health.

Protect your eyes from harmful UV rays by wearing sunglasses when outdoors, especially on sunny days.

Follow the 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, take a 20-second break, and look at something 20 feet away to reduce eye strain.

Avoid prolonged screen time and remember to blink regularly to keep your eyes moist.

Get regular eye check-ups to detect any vision problems early and prevent potential complications.

Avoid smoking, as it can increase the risk of developing age-related macular degeneration and other eye diseases.

Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day to maintain optimal eye function.

Practice good hygiene by washing your hands before touching your eyes to prevent infections.
